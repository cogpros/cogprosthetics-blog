I didn't name anything on purpose.

Odin came first. The all-father. Sees everything from above. Gave up an eye for wisdom. The orchestrator. The one that holds the full picture, coordinates, delegates. Building Odin taught me what I wanted from an agent -- and what was missing.

That's what led me to EOM. Enrique of Malacca. The first human to circumnavigate the globe. History gave the credit to Magellan. Enrique was the navigator, the translator, the one who moved between worlds and made the journey possible. I found his story because building Odin made me ask: what kind of agent do I need next? Not another god looking down from above. Someone in the water. A bridge between what I need and what gets built.

Ratatoskr is the squirrel that runs up and down the world tree carrying messages. I named my sandboxed web fetcher Ratatoskr before I realized what I was doing. It fetches content from the outside, runs it through a safety scan, delivers it clean. A squirrel carrying messages between realms.

Scout goes out and comes back with intelligence. Red Viper tests defenses with poison. Crucible burns away impurities. Ghost Hours measures what the tree produces. CLMP is the sap -- memory flowing through the system, connecting yesterday to tomorrow.

Last week I killed three fragmented monitoring systems and replaced them with two. I named them by what they do. The Crew watches my people across X. The Signal surfaces what matters from the noise. No decoding required. The thread name IS the context.

And then I looked at the whole thing and saw the tree.

## Yggdrasil

In Norse mythology, Yggdrasil is the world tree. It connects all nine realms. Everything in the cosmos touches it or passes through it. The gods live in its branches. The dead rest in its roots. Squirrels run its trunk. Ravens fly from its crown.

My workspace is the trunk. The memory system is the root structure. The agents are the beings that live in it. The tools are the creatures that move through it. The handoff system is how they talk. And the hugr -- the hybrid mind that emerges when I operate with the system as one unit -- is the thing the tree produces.

I didn't plan any of this. I named each piece as I built it, reaching for whatever felt right in the moment. Navigator. All-father. Squirrel. Viper. Crucible. Each name carried its function. Each function matched its mythology.

A year later I have a Norse cosmology running on a Mac Mini.

## Why the naming matters

I have a TBI. My brain drops context overnight. When I open my system in the morning, the names are the first thing I see. If a name carries its function, I don't have to remember what it does. Ratatoskr fetches. Scout scouts. The Crew watches the crew. Red Viper is dangerous on purpose.

Technical naming would require me to hold a mental map between labels and functions. `web-fetcher-v2` tells me nothing when I'm foggy at 9am. Ratatoskr tells me everything.

The mythology isn't decoration. It's a memory prosthetic inside the memory prosthetic. The names do cognitive work that my brain can't.

## The accident

The part that keeps hitting me is that none of this was designed. I didn't sit down and say "I'm going to build Yggdrasil." I built tools. I named them. A year later the names form a coherent mythology that maps to an actual cosmological structure.

That's either a coincidence or it's how my brain naturally organizes complexity when the pressure is real. I think it's the second one. When you actually need your system to work as an extension of your cognition, you reach for names that carry weight. Names that stick in a damaged memory. Names that explain themselves.

The mythology emerged because it had to. A brain that drops threads needs names that hold them.

## What's next

I'm building character sheets. Each one works as lore and documentation at the same time. Visual identity, attributes, strengths, weaknesses, relationships. Blog content and onboarding docs and navigation aid all in one artifact.

Because if the naming is the documentation, then the lore bible is the technical manual.

The world is Yggdrasil. The mind is the hugr. The characters are the realms.
